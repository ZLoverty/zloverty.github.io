Intensity autocorrelations

The concentration (in our case image intensity) shows long tail, decaying logarithmically rather than the much more rapid $t^{-1}$ decay, which happens in thermal systems. This intrinsic time scale, which varies with concentration, makes the analysis on $\frac{\partial c}{\partial t}$ not simply the subtraction between adjacent frames. In order to choose an appropriate time interval, we analyze the autocorrelation of intensity at 20, 40, 60, 80 and 100 n$_0$.

Comments

The characteristic time scale increases with concentration. The variance of the correlation time across the field of view also increases. For high concentrations, such as 80 and 100 n$_0$, which are of our interest, we find 10 frames is a good time interval, because most autocorrelation drops to 0 around 10 frames. Too long time interval can result in random results.
